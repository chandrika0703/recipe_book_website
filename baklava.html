BAKLAVA

History

Baklava, a rich, sweet pastry, traces its origins to the imperial kitchens of the Ottoman Empire, evolving from ancient Middle Eastern sweets. Its exact origin is debated, with some attributing it to the Assyrians. Layers of filo dough, nuts (usually pistachios or walnuts), and syrup or honey make up its iconic structure. Throughout history, Baklava spread across the Mediterranean and Middle East, becoming a beloved dessert in Greek, Turkish, and Arabic cuisines. Its popularity grew globally with variations emerging in different cultures. Today, baklava remains a symbol of indulgence, tradition, and cultural exchange, delighting palates worldwide.




Ingredients

  • 1 package phyllo dough
  • 4 c. chopped walnuts or pecans
  • 1 tsp. cinnamon
  • 1 1/2 sticks butter
  • 2 c. honey
  • 1/2 c. water
  • 1/2 c. sugar
  • 3 tsp. vanilla extract

Method

Step-1:Remove phyllo dough package from freezer and place in the fridge for 24 hours to thaw. Remove from fridge 1 hour before using.

Step-2:When working with the phyllo dough, only remove the sheets you immediately need, keeping the other sheets covered in plastic wrap, then a damp cloth.

Step-3:Toss together the chopped walnuts and cinnamon. Set aside.

Step-4:Preheat oven to 350Â°F. Melt 1/2 stick of the butter in a small saucepan and butter a rectangular baking pan. Make sure the sheets of phyllo will generally fit the pan (if they're a little bigger, that's okay.) If they're much bigger, just trim them with a sharp knife.

Step-5:Butter the top sheet of phyllo with melted butter, then grab it and the unbuttered sheet below it. Set the 2 sheets in the pan, buttered sheet face down. Press lightly into the pan. Repeat this twice more, so that you have 6 sheets of phyllo in the pan, three of the sheets buttered.

Step-6:Sprinkle on enough walnuts to make a single layer. Butter 2 sheets of phyllo and place them on top of the walnuts. Add more walnuts, then 2 more buttered phyllo sheets. Repeat this a couple more times, or until you're out of walnuts. Top with 4 more buttered phyllo sheets, ending with a buttered top. Cut a diagonal diamond pattern in the baklava using a very sharp knife.

Step-7:Bake for 45 minutes, or until the baklava is very golden brown.

Step-8:While the baklava is baking, combine the remaining 1 stick of butter, honey, water, sugar, and vanilla in a saucepan. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low.

Step-9:When you remove the baklava from the oven, drizzle half the saucepan evenly all over the top. Allow it to sit and absorb for a minute, then drizzle on a little more until you think it's thoroughly moistened. You'll likely have some of the honey mixture leftover, which you can drink with a straw. Just kidding.

Step-10:Allow the baklava to cool, uncovered, for several hours. Once cool and sticky and divine, carefully remove them from the pan and serve with coffee (or give as gifts!)
