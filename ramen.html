JAPANESE RAMEN

History

Ramen, a Japanese noodle soup dish, has a rich history tracing back to the late 19th century. Originally introduced to Japan by Chinese immigrants, ramen evolved into a distinct culinary phenomenon. Its popularity surged after World War II when food shortages led to the widespread consumption of wheat noodles. Instant ramen, invented by Momofuku Ando in 1958, revolutionized ramen culture, making it accessible worldwide. Ramen shops, or "ramen-ya," proliferated, each boasting unique broth, noodle, and topping combinations. Today, ramen stands as a beloved symbol of Japanese culinary innovation, with regional variations and global acclaim for its comforting and flavorful qualities.




Ingredients

  • 2 chicken breasts
  • kosher salt and freshly-ground black pepper, to season
  • 1 Tbsp unsalted butter
  • 2 tsp sesame or vegetable oil
  • 2 tsp fresh ginger, minced
  • 1 Tbsp fresh garlic, minced
  • 3 Tbsp low-sodium soy sauce
  • 2 Tbsp mirin
  • 4 cups rich chicken stock
  • 1 oz dried shitake mushrooms
  • 1–2 tsp sea salt, to taste
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup scallions, sliced
  • 2 (3 oz) packs dried ramen noodles

Method

COOK THE CHICKEN:

Step-1:Preheat the oven to 375℉. Season the chicken generously with salt and pepper.

Step-2:Melt the butter in a large oven-safe skillet over medium heat. Add the chicken, skin-side down, and cook until the skin is golden brown and releases easily from the pan, about 5-7 minutes. Flip the chicken over and cook for another 4-5 minutes, until golden.

Step-3:Transfer the skillet to the oven and roast for 15-20 minutes, until the chicken is cooked through. Remove from the oven, transfer the chicken to a plate, and cover with foil until ready to serve.

MAKE THE RAMEN BROTH:

Step-1:Heat the oil in a large pot over medium heat until shimmering. Add the garlic and ginger, and cook for a few minutes until softened. Add the soy sauce and mirin, and stir to combine. Cook for another minute.

Step-2:Add the stock, cover, and bring to a boil. Remove the lid, and let simmer uncovered for 5 minutes, then add the dried mushrooms. Simmer gently for another 10 minutes, and season with salt, to taste.

MAKE THE SOFT-BOILED EGGS:

Step-1:Fill a pot with enough water to cover the eggs, and bring to a boil. Gently lower the eggs (still cold from the fridge) into the boiling water, and let simmer for 7 minutes (for a slightly-runny yolk) or 8 minutes (for a soft, but set-up yolk).

Step-2:Meanwhile, fill a large bowl with ice water. When the timer finishes, transfer the eggs to the ice bath to stop the cooking process. Wait at least 5 minutes, or until cool enough to handle, then carefully peel away the shell and slice in half, lengthwise. Set aside until ready to serve.

ASSEMBLE THE RAMEN BOWLS:

Step-1:Meanwhile, chop the scallions and jalapeño (if using). Slice the chicken into thin pieces. Set aside. When the eggs finish cooking, add the ramen noodles to the boiling water.

Step-2:Cook for 2-3 minutes, until soft, then divide the noodles into two large bowls. Add the sliced chicken and the ramen broth. Top with the fresh scallions, jalapeño, and the soft boiled egg. Serve immediately. Enjoy!!
